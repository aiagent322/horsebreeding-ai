About Mister Montana Nic

Mister Montana Nic — 1998 Bay Stallion, AQHA, 15 hands. Standing at Silver Spurs Equine, Scottsdale AZ. Full breeding information, video, and booking available at silverspursequine.com/mister-montana-nic-1.

By Reminic (NRHA Hall of Fame; NRHA $8M+ Million Dollar Sire; NRCHA Hall of Fame; NRCHA #5 All-Time Leading Sire; NRHA #9 All-Time Leading Sire; Equi-Stat All-Time Leading Reined Cow Horse Sire; 1993 International Reining World Cup Open & Team Champion; AQHA Junior Reining World Champion; NRHA Derby Champion; PE $9.5M+; sire of Boomernic, Nic It In The Bud, Von Reminic, Reminics Pep, and Mister Montana Nic — five earners over $100,000 from one foundational sire line) out of Mollie Montana (1988 AQHA Bay Mare — NCHA money-earner; World Show Qualifier; dam of NRHA and NRCHA money-earners and AQHA Performance Point earners; her offspring have competed across reining, reined cow horse, and cutting, confirming the cross-discipline versatility she contributes to Mister Montana Nic's pedigree).

Mister Montana Nic's career spans nearly a decade of elite-level competition across domestic and international reining — a longevity matched by very few horses at the premium open level. He debuted at the 2001 NRHA Open Futurity as a three-year-old and placed 7th. He returned to major competition in 2004 and 2005, winning the CRIO NRHA Futurity Gold Medal and earning the FEI World Reining Masters Silver Medal in Manerbio, Italy in 2005 — establishing him as a legitimate international competitor. In 2006 he won the NRBC $25,000 Open Reserve Championship, one of the most valuable checks in reining at the time.

The defining chapter of his career came in 2010 with Craig Schmersal — NRHA Multi-Million Dollar Rider — in the saddle. The pair won the 2010 AQHA Senior Reining World Championship and the 2010 Florida Reining Horse Classic Open Championship. At the 2010 FEI World Equestrian Games held in Lexington, Kentucky — the sport's highest international stage, held only once every four years — Mister Montana Nic and Schmersal contributed to the United States Team Gold Medal victory and earned the Individual Silver Medal. That combination of a domestic World Championship and an international WEG medal in the same season is among the most accomplished single years any Silver Spurs stallion has posted. He continued competing through 2010, winning multiple NRHA Rookie division titles and placing 4th at the Battle in the Saddle WEG qualifier alongside Tim McQuay. His $183,354 LTE reflects one of the deeper career earnings records in the Silver Spurs stallion lineup. Full information and booking at silverspursequine.com/mister-montana-nic-1.
