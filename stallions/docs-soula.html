About Docs Soula

Docs Soula — 2003 Sorrel Stallion, AQHA registered, 14.3 hands. Standing at Silver Spurs Equine, Scottsdale AZ. Full breeding information, video, and booking available at silverspursequine.com/docs-soula-1.

By Soula Jule Star (LTE $129,222; NRCHA Non-Pro Derby Reserve Champion; Abilene Spectacular Non-Pro Derby Champion; Suncoast Winter Non-Pro Derby Champion; 2012 NRCHA Top 20 Leading Sire; NRCHA All-Time Leading Sire; offspring earnings $1,100,000+) out of Docs Hickory Nut — a dam line carrying Doc Bar bloodlines through Doc's Hickory, one of the foundational cutting horse sires in western performance horse history. Docs Hickory Nut is also the dam of Doc At Night, the 2002 NRCHA Snaffle Bit Futurity Champion — making Docs Soula a maternal half-brother to an NRCHA Futurity Champion. The Docs Hickory Nut family represents one of the most productive cow horse dam lines of the early 2000s, producing both a Futurity Champion (Doc At Night) and a Derby Champion (Docs Soula) from the same mare.

"He was a 75 cutter, a 75 down the fence, and he could score bigger than that in the reining. And at the end of the day, you'd put him in his stall, and he would lay down flat and be snoring in about five minutes." — Doug Williamson, NRCHA Million Dollar Rider

Docs Soula's career was built on one of the rarest qualities in a cow horse: true cross-discipline balance. His rider Doug Williamson — an NRCHA Million Dollar Rider who won the 2002 NRCHA Snaffle Bit Futurity, the 2011 NRCHA Open Bridle World Championship, and numerous other major titles — consistently described Docs Soula as a horse anyone could ride: willing, accurate, and capable of putting up big scores in the cutting pen, down the fence, and in the reining. In 2007 the pair won the NRCHA Western Derby Open Championship, then came back in 2008 to take the AQHA World Show Reserve Jr. Working Cow Horse Championship — a combination that proved his all-around horsemanship credentials. When Williamson was injured in 2008, he trusted the stallion to fellow NRCHA Million Dollar Rider Bob Avila for the AQHA World Show, a testament to the horse's trainable, professional temperament.

With a lifetime earnings record of $150,000, 132 AQHA Performance Points across cutting, reining, and working cow horse, and titles at both the NRCHA Derby and the AQHA World Show, Docs Soula carries a proven competition résumé that punches well above his current breeding fee. He is a maternal half-brother to an NRCHA Snaffle Bit Futurity Champion, trained by one of the sport's most decorated professionals, and sired by an NRCHA All-Time Leading Sire — a pedigree and performance combination that positions him as one of the deeper values in the Silver Spurs program. Offspring earnings of $85,000+ confirm early producer ability. Breeders targeting cow horse, cutting, or all-around versatility at an accessible fee will find Docs Soula a compelling option.
