About Nic It In The Bud

Nic It In The Bud — 1997 Bay Stallion, AQHA, 14.2 hands. Standing at Silver Spurs Equine, Scottsdale AZ. Full breeding information, video, and booking available at silverspursequine.com/nic-it-in-the-bud-1.

By Reminic (NRHA Hall of Fame; NRHA $8M+ Million Dollar Sire; NRCHA Hall of Fame; NRCHA #5 All-Time Leading Sire; NRHA #9 All-Time Leading Sire; Equi-Stat All-Time Leading Reined Cow Horse Sire; 1993 International Reining World Cup Open & Team Champion; AQHA Junior Reining World Champion; NRHA Derby Champion; offspring earnings $9.5M+, including producers with get earnings $16M+; sire of Von Reminic $180,219 [NRHA Open Futurity Champion], Nic It In The Bud, Reminics Pep $135,463 [NRCHA Open Futurity Champion], Mister Montana Nic $116,792, and Boomernic $113,128 [NRHA Open Futurity Champion] — five top performers over $100,000, all world-class competitors) out of Genuine Redbud (by Genuine Doc — 1,345 AQHA points; $61,467 in earnings; 20 AQHA High Point titles; High Point All-Around Champion twice; High Point All-Around Mare; World Show Superhorse; World Champion Junior Heading Horse; Reserve World Champion Junior and Senior Calf Roping Horse; High Point titles in Calf Roping, Heading, and Heeling — one of the most decorated all-around performance mares in AQHA history and the source of the athletic versatility and brilliant mind that defines Nic It In The Bud's cross-discipline range).

Trained and shown throughout his entire career by NRCHA Million Dollar Rider Todd Bergen, Nic It In The Bud made his debut at the 2000 NRCHA Snaffle Bit Futurity in Reno, qualifying for the Open finals. Less than three months later he traveled to the NRHA Futurity in Oklahoma City and again earned a berth in the Open finals — back-to-back futurity finalists in two different associations in the same season. As a four-year-old in 2001, he dominated the NRCHA aged event circuit, winning both the Stakes Open Championship and the Derby Open Championship — a double that confirms elite consistency, not a single peak. He was also an AQHA World Championship finalist in Junior Reining that year.

"All I have showed him in this year is cutting. I didn't really know how he was going to be. I only did reining on him for two days before we left to come here!" — Todd Bergen, after winning the 2002 NRCHA Woodside Derby

Bergen's comment after the 2002 Woodside Derby tells the complete story of this horse's natural ability: two days of reining preparation, then a Derby Championship. When he advanced to bridle horse age in 2003, Nic It In The Bud proved he could sustain elite performance at any stage of competition, earning the World's Greatest Horseman Reserve Championship and finishing top 10 at the AQHA World Show in Senior Working Cow Horse. He returned to WGH in 2007 and won the Preliminary Rein Work Championship — a long career defined by consistent excellence from futurity through aged events. He retired with lifetime earnings of approximately $148,165 across reining, reined cow horse, and cutting.

As a sire, Nic It In The Bud became the 13th horse to achieve NRCHA Million Dollar Sire status, confirmed in 2014 alongside Dual Rey. His reined cow horse offspring have earned $1,001,722+ (NRCHA confirmed). Combined NRHA and NRCHA offspring earnings total $2,723,993+ per Stallion Central. He reached top 12 NRCHA sire and top 30 NRHA sire rankings in 2009, then climbed to top 10 in both associations in 2011. The Reminic x Genuine Doc pedigree — two Hall of Fame sire lines combining NRHA supremacy with AQHA all-around athleticism and intelligence — continues producing versatile, competitive offspring across cow horse and reining disciplines. Full information and booking at silverspursequine.com/nic-it-in-the-bud-1.
