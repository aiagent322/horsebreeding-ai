About Einsteins Revolution

Einsteins Revolution — 2002 Dun Stallion, AQHA, 14.2 hands. Known throughout his career as "Junior." Bred by Christian and Rosi Wagner of Germany. Owned by Silver Spurs Equine LLC in partnership with Ruben Vandorp. Frozen semen available — contact Silver Spurs Equine at silverspursequine.com/einsteins-revolution. Einsteins Revolution passed away in 2026, leaving behind an NRHA $2 Million sire record and one of the most celebrated competitive careers in the sport's history.

By Great Resolve (aka Einstein — LTE $147,377; Congress Open Reining Futurity Champion; 3rd NRHA Open Futurity; NRHA Million Dollar Sire; 2011 NRHA Top 25 Leading Sire) out of Fly Flashy Jac (by Boggies Flashy Jac — AQHA point-earner, Atlantic Breeders Main Event Novice Horse Open placing). The combination of Great Resolve's competitive reining genetics on top and the Boggies Flashy Jac foundation below created an individual whose natural athleticism and trainable, dynamic mind made him one of the most attractive stallions to the top reining professionals of his era — Tim McQuay, Shawn Flarida, and Ruben Vandorp all showed him at major events, a measure of confidence in a single horse that is almost without precedent.

"PHYSICIST ALBERT EINSTEIN'S GREATEST INSIGHT WAS that mass and energy are two sides of the same coin. In the world of Reining, the proof was Einsteins Revolution, a dynamic stallion that turned mass into energy, and energy into cash." — The Reiner, August 2015

Einsteins Revolution's show career is defined by a milestone that stands alone in NRHA history: he was the first horse ever to exceed $300,000 in lifetime NRHA earnings, a barrier that had never been crossed before him. He did it in a career managed unusually by owner/trainer Ruben Vandorp, who strategically deployed three of the sport's best riders across different events. Tim McQuay showed him to an L4 Open Finalist finish at the 2005 NRHA Futurity. Shawn Flarida — the leading money-earning rider in the sport — took him to the 2006 NRBC and won the Level 4 Open Championship in the final run of the contest with "a performance for the ages," pocketing a $63,000 check. Vandorp then showed him himself at subsequent events, winning the 2007 NRBC L3 Open (tying Fappani at 232 in the process before losing a ride-off) and the 2007 NRHA Derby L3 Open Championship. He also won both the 2007 and 2008 Bill Horn Shootout events at $30,000 each — $60,000 in a single two-year stretch from a single competition format.

In 2006 and 2007 he was Equi-Stat's #1 Leading Reining Money Earner — back-to-back across two separate seasons. His final LTE of $355,500 includes an AQHA ROM and 46 reining points. As a sire, he reached the NRHA Million Dollar milestone in June 2015 and the NRHA $2 Million milestone in October 2018. Total offspring earnings now stand at $2,004,006+ with confirmed top earners Turn Down For What ($183,359), Revolution Is Dun ($168,051), Footwork Revolution ($132,215), and Snip O Lution ($109,050).
